# Junior Data Analyst
## Case Study Interview Assignment

---

## Purpose of This Assignment

This exercise simulates the type of work you would do as a **Junior Data Analyst** in this role.

There is **no single correct answer**.

We are evaluating:

- How you approach a business problem
- How you explore and reason with data
- How you perform basic analysis
- How clearly you communicate insights

> **Note:**  
> This is **not** a test of advanced analytics or complex modeling.  
> This is a test of **foundational data skills**, curiosity, and business understanding.

---

## Business Context

You have joined the analytics team at a **manufacturing and retail company** that manages inventory across multiple product categories and warehouses.

Leadership has observed:

- Frequent **stock-outs** for some products
- **Excess inventory** for other products
- Limited visibility into which products require **immediate attention**

The business wants to **optimize inventory management** to reduce waste, avoid stock-outs, and improve operational efficiency.

You have been given access to the **AdventureWorks** dataset to help understand what is happening, why it is happening, and what actions should be considered.

---

## Data Provided

You will be given an **AdventureWorks database backup (`.bak` file)** containing data related to:

- Products
- Product categories and subcategories
- Inventory levels
- Sales and order history
- Dates and locations

---

## Your Task

Using the provided data, analyze **inventory performance** and support better decision-making by addressing the following:

---

### 1. Understand What Is Happening

- Which products or categories have the **highest** and **lowest** inventory levels?
- Which products **sell the most**, and which **sell the least**?
- Are there products with **high inventory but low sales**?
- Are there products with **low inventory but high sales**?
- What trends or patterns stand out?

---

### 2. Diagnose Why It’s Happening

- How do **sales patterns** relate to inventory levels?
- Are there meaningful differences by **product category or subcategory**?
- Are certain products consistently **overstocked** or at risk of **stock-outs**?
- Where does the data limit your conclusions?

---

### 3. Provide Business Intelligence

- What are the **top 2–3 insights** leadership should care about right now?
- Which products or categories would you **prioritize for action**?
- What **simple recommendations** would you make to improve inventory management?

> You are encouraged to **challenge assumptions** if the data does not support them.

---

## Deliverables

Please submit the following:

---

### 1. Analysis Work

- SQL queries used to explore the data
- Any Excel analysis used to summarize findings
- Clear comments explaining logic and assumptions

---

### 2. Insight Summary (Maximum 1 Page)

- Written for a **non-technical audience**
- Focused on insights, implications, and recommendations
- Avoid unnecessary technical detail

---

### 3. Optional Visuals

- Simple tables or charts if they improve clarity
- Dashboards are optional and **not required**

## Data Access Instructions

Here's link on how to access and install **AdventureWorks `.bak` file**.

[How to Download and Install the AdventureWorks Database in SQL Server](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/installation-guide/how-to-download-and-install-adventureworks-database-in-sql/)



### How to Access the Data

1. Open **SQL Server Management Studio (SSMS)**
2. Connect to your local SQL Server instance
3. Right-click on **Databases → Restore Database**
4. Select **Device** and upload the provided `.bak` file
5. Restore the **AdventureWorks** database
6. Run `SELECT` queries to explore the data

> You may query the data, but **do not modify or delete** any tables.
